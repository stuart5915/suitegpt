TL;DR
Two particles become linked so that measuring one instantly tells you about the other, no matter how far apart they are ‚Äî like a pair of magic coins that always land on opposite sides.

The Spooky Connection

When two particles become entangled, they share a quantum state. Measure one and you instantly know the state of the other ‚Äî even if it's on the other side of the universe. Einstein famously called this "spooky action at a distance."

üîó Analogy: Imagine putting one glove from a pair in a box and shipping it to Mars. When you open your box and see a left glove, you instantly know Mars has the right glove ‚Äî except in quantum mechanics, neither glove "decides" which hand it fits until you look.

Why It Matters

Quantum entanglement isn't just a curiosity ‚Äî it's the backbone of quantum computing and quantum cryptography. Entangled particles enable quantum computers to process information in ways classical computers simply cannot.
